Generalized Tobit Model

In econometrics, a "censored" dataset is one where some y values have been truncated. This can estimation difficult.

One example is income; in many datasets, income data is capped at a certain level for privacy. Therefore, a wealthy individual may appear as having an income of $250,000, when in reality they are much wealthier. Therefore, we'd expect every observation to have the following property:

$$y_i \leq 250000 \quad \forall i $$

Some datasets may have different thresholds for different datasets. For example, if you are collecting data on the length of unemployment for people, individuals who have not yet found a job are censored in the sense that their true length of unemployment is longer than what the survey records. In this sense, censored observations have a threshold value that is unique to that observation. We denote this threshold value $T_i$.
